Trust Center

We secure the record of what your AI did.

Obrioxia is built on a "Zero Trust" philosophy. We assume that systems can be compromised, and we design our ledger to provide forensic evidence even in adversarial conditions.

Encryption Standards

We utilise TLS 1.3 for all data in transit. Data at rest is protected using envelope encryption with industry-standard authenticated ciphers. When configured in write-only mode, our crypto-shredding engine uses per-event sealing to ensure the production service can encrypt data but never decrypt historical records.

Tamper-Evident Cryptographic Chaining

Every decision event is cryptographically chained to its predecessor using SHA-256 hashing. Any modification — even a single character — breaks the chain and is immediately detectable. The chain is linear, rooted at a genesis block, and enforced by database-level unique constraints on each link.

Privacy & Right to Erasure

We resolve the conflict between "Tamper-Evident Logs" and "Right to be Forgotten." By encrypting PII with write-only per-event keys before hashing, we allow for the cryptographic destruction of personal data. When a record's key material is destroyed, PII access is permanently revoked and the data is rendered cryptographically irrecoverable, while the audit trail remains intact and verifiable.

Data Residency & Retention

Obrioxia manages data retention through cryptographic access revocation to align with your legal jurisdiction. Retention periods vary by subscription tier. Contact us for details on long-term archival options.

Access Control

Multiple authentication modes are supported, including customer API keys, Firebase-verified bearer tokens, and scoped demo keys. Admin operations require elevated credentials. Feature access is gated by subscription tier through the entitlement system.

Crypto-Shredding

Obrioxia reconciles immutable audit logs with data-erasure obligations through cryptographic key destruction. When a subject exercises their Right to Erasure, the wrapped per-event encryption key is permanently deleted from the key store. The encrypted data remains in the ledger but becomes irrecoverable. Chain verification continues to pass because the hash chain does not depend on the encryption key material.
