Trust Center

Regulatory-grade evidence infrastructure for AI decision records.

Obrioxia is built on a "Zero Trust" philosophy. We assume that systems can be compromised, and we design our ledger to provide forensic evidence even in adversarial conditions.

Encryption Standards

All data in transit is protected with TLS 1.3. Data at rest is protected using per-event envelope encryption: AES-256-GCM with a unique Data Encryption Key (DEK) per record, wrapped with RSA-OAEP using the writer's public key. The production service holds only the public key — it can seal records but never decrypt historical data.

Tamper-Evident Cryptographic Chaining

Every decision event is cryptographically chained to its predecessor using SHA-256 hashing. Any modification — even a single character — breaks the chain and is immediately detectable. The chain is linear, rooted at a genesis block, with uniqueness enforced via indexed constraints on each link.

Privacy & Right to Erasure

We resolve the conflict between "Tamper-Evident Logs" and "Right to be Forgotten." By encrypting PII with write-only per-event keys before hashing, we allow for the cryptographic destruction of personal data. When a record's key material is destroyed, PII access is permanently revoked and the data is rendered cryptographically irrecoverable, while the audit trail remains intact and verifiable.

Data Residency & Retention

Obrioxia manages data retention through cryptographic access revocation to align with your legal jurisdiction. Retention periods vary by subscription tier. Contact us for details on long-term archival options.

Access Control

Multiple authentication modes are supported, including customer API keys, Firebase-verified bearer tokens, and scoped demo keys. Admin operations require elevated credentials. Feature access is gated by subscription tier through the entitlement system.

Crypto-Shredding

Obrioxia reconciles immutable audit logs with data-erasure obligations through cryptographic key destruction. When a subject exercises their Right to Erasure, the per-event DEK wrapped on the record is permanently nulled. The encrypted data remains in the ledger but becomes cryptographically irrecoverable. Chain verification continues to pass because the hash computation explicitly excludes key material.

Retention Enforcement

Retention periods are enforced cryptographically according to the active subscription plan. When a record exceeds its retention window, the wrapped encryption key associated with that record is permanently destroyed. Encrypted data remains part of the tamper-evident chain, but becomes mathematically irrecoverable. Retention is enforced at the evidence layer, not merely defined in policy.
